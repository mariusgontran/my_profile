My Interest in IT

My Interest in IT

One of the most enjoyable parts of my career has been using my problem-solving skills whenever faced with a challenge. However, I have often found that my ability to resolve issues were limited by the function of the tools I had access to at the time. Therefore, my interest in IT grew more and more every time I faced these situations where I knew that either another tool could do the job better or that it is entirely possible to create the right tool for the task with the right skills.


When did my interest in IT start?

Like most people nowadays, I was exposed to computers from a very young age. To entertain myself, I would spend long hours sitting down at my desk and using different applications to see what they could do and playing any computer games I would come across. As a result, I grew up seeing multiple technologies implemented in the IT world, and I witnessed how we all changed our interactions with computers to reach where we are today. From the adoption of smartphones and social media to the various IT solutions implemented in most industries, there has been a massive shift in using computers in our daily lives. I am delighted to have been following this since I was young.


A particular event that sparked my interest

One of the main events that encouraged me to pursue my studies in IT was my involvement with a significant system overhaul that happened while I was working for a large retailer in their supply chain department. The system overhaul introduced multiple new applications to work with our current systems. I was very interested in watching how the IT engineering team first mapped out how all the existing applications worked and how users would use them in real-life scenarios. Then, they analysed the best solutions and consulted us before going ahead and putting in place the new applications and the accompanying reworked processes. I was amazed at how they worked in a team to make this whole experience as painless as possible for the users while working in the background to program the required software and APIs to ensure all would work seamlessly once in place. It showed me how it becomes much easier to respond to these challenges with the right skills and IT tools.


Why did I choose to come to RMIT?

After comparing the various IT undergraduate degrees offered in Melbourne and speaking with relatives and friends at multiple universities, I decided to apply to RMIT to complete my Bachelor of IT via Open Universities. I preferred to study online as it offered complete flexibility with my current work and social arrangements while receiving education recognised by the industry.


What I expect to learn during my studies

With completing my Bachelor, I will obtain skills that will allow me to become an IT Professional with expertise in various disciplines. With my first two subjects, Introduction to IT and Introduction to Programming, I will be taking the first steps in learning the fundamentals to achieve my goal. In addition, Introduction to IT will show me a range of IT fields, programs and competencies required to start my IT career (RMIT n.d.). Meanwhile, in Introduction to Programming, I will be learning the essentials parts of programming and how to think in step-by-step to resolve a problem then matching this to a program (RMIT n.d.). In the rest of the course, I will learn to further develop my knowledge in IT and learn more about other interesting IT areas.

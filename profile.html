    What do these test results mean?

    While some of the traits from the different test contradict themselves, they are vital points to which I can relate and agree. For example, there is no arguing that I am naturally more introverted, as described by both the Myers-Briggs and the Big Five Personality test results. I also tend to make decisions more rationally based on facts and analysis rather than on my intuition or feelings alone, as shown in the Myers-Briggs test results. The prospecting trait in these results also correctly shows how flexible I can be with dealing with unexpected challenges, enhancing my problem-solving skills. Knowing that I have a visual learning style will help me choose the best way to take in information when required and understand my most robust method of communication.

    How will these results influence my behaviour in a team?

    I believe my agreeableness will have a very positive effect on any teamwork requiring done. I enjoy helping others achieve the best they can as much as myself doing it. The openness trait brings creativity that is often needed when facing new challenges, while conscientiousness will help me exercise the self-motivation required to meet the team's deadlines. Though I scored low on extraversion, this is an area where I make conscious efforts to improve communicating with others when required, as I am fully aware that not doing so will only be adverse to the team.

    How should I take this into account when forming a team?

    When forming the team, these results will help us determine our overall strengths and weaknesses. We will take the time to compare our results and identify the key areas where we do not have complimentary qualities to ensure we develop those throughout our group work. It will be an excellent opportunity for all members to work side-by-side to either share their trait or work towards improving certain areas together.
